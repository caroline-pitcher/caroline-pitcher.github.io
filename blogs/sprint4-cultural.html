Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

It helps people understand that there isn’t a limit to what their brain can achieve as the brain is constantly changing and developing. If you start learning something and keep practicing it your brain will develop new pathways and connections and make them stronger. If you stop doing something, after a while your brain will prune those connections. Neuroplasticity helps remove the limits we put on ourselves and even if something has affected your brain like a physical or mental illness or injury, it can help you rebuild and develop new brain patterns.

What is Growth Mindset and why is it relevant

It’s similar to neuroplasticity in that it says there is no limit on what you can learn or achieve, or rather the limit is one you have placed on yourself if you have a fixed mindset. A growth mindset means that you believe you are capable of improvement, that you can learn something and get better at it at any time. A fixed mindset is one where you think you either have an innate trait or talent for something or you don’t and that’s it, there is no possibility of change or growth.
